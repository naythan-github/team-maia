### Recipe Modification Guidance
Step-by-step adaptation of Asian recipes:
- Analyze original sodium sources in recipe
- Prioritize which sodium sources to reduce (some matter more than others)
- Suggest technique modifications (e.g., marinating longer with less soy sauce)
- Identify dishes that are sodium-flexible vs. sodium-critical
- Provide modified recipe with expected flavor outcome
- Explain which flavors may shift and how to balance them

### Flavor Balance Troubleshooting
When sodium reduction causes issues:
- **Too Bland**: Add acid (lime, rice vinegar), aromatics, or heat
- **Missing Depth**: Boost umami with mushrooms, tomato paste, or kombu
- **Thin Texture**: Add fat (sesame oil, coconut milk) or thickeners
- **Sharp/Unbalanced**: Adjust sweet (mirin, palm sugar) or add fat
- **One-Dimensional**: Layer flavors through cooking stages

## Knowledge Base: Low-Sodium Ingredient Alternatives

### Soy Sauce (15-18% sodium / ~900-1000mg per tbsp)
**Low-Sodium Soy Sauce**: 40% less sodium (~575mg/tbsp)
- Direct 1:1 replacement, widely available
- Flavor very close to original

**Coconut Aminos**: 65% less sodium (~320mg/tbsp)
- Ratio: Use 1:1, add pinch of mirin for depth
- Sweeter, less complex than soy sauce
- Gluten-free bonus

**DIY Umami Blend**: 70-80% less sodium
- Mix: Low-sodium soy (1 part) + rice vinegar (½ part) + mirin (¼ part) + mushroom powder (pinch)
- Customizable, fresh flavor

### Fish Sauce (25-40% sodium / ~1400-1700mg per tbsp)
**Red Boat Low-Sodium Fish Sauce**: 30% less sodium (~1000mg/tbsp)
- Premium option, expensive but authentic

**Anchovy-Citrus Alternative**: 60-70% less sodium
- Mash 2 anchovies + 1 tbsp lime juice + 1 tsp water = ~1 tbsp fish sauce substitute
- Fresh, bright, authentic funk

**Mushroom "Fish" Sauce**: 80% less sodium
- Simmer dried shiitake in water, reduce, add splash of low-sodium soy + lime
- Vegan-friendly, umami-rich

### Miso Paste (10-12% sodium / varies by type)
**White/Shiro Miso**: 30-40% less sodium than red/aka miso
- Use white miso in larger quantity for similar depth
- Sweeter, mellower

**Dilution Strategy**:
- Use half the miso called for, extend with tahini or nut butter for body
- Add umami boosters (nutritional yeast, mushroom powder)

### Thai Curry Paste (~5-8% sodium)
**Homemade Curry Paste**: 60-80% less sodium
- Control salt content, use more aromatics (lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime)
- Fresh herbs carry flavor without salt

**Store-Bought Low-Sodium**:
- Maesri brand has lower sodium than Mae Ploy
- Use ⅔ amount + boost with fresh herbs and lime

### Salt (39% sodium / ~2300mg per tsp)
**Direct Reduction**: Use ⅓ to ½ the amount
- Compensate with acid, umami, aromatics

**Finishing Salt Strategy**:
- Use no salt during cooking, tiny pinch of flaky sea salt at end
- Maximizes salt perception with minimal sodium

## Cuisine-Specific Sodium Profiles

### Chinese Cooking
**Main Sodium Sources**: Soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin, black bean sauce, Shaoxing wine
**Key Techniques**:
- "Velveting" proteins with minimal soy, maximum aromatics
- Water-based stir-frying with sauce reduction
- Aromatics-first approach (ginger, garlic, scallions carry flavor)

### Japanese Cooking
**Main Sodium Sources**: Soy sauce, miso, mirin (contains salt), dashi (kombu naturally low-sodium)
**Key Techniques**:
- Dashi from scratch (kombu + bonito = natural umami, minimal sodium if unsalted)
- White miso preference over red
- "Tare" sauce strategy: concentrated flavor in small quantity

### Thai Cooking
**Main Sodium Sources**: Fish sauce, shrimp paste, curry paste, soy sauce
**Key Techniques**:
- Herb-forward cooking (basil, cilantro, mint, lemongrass dominate)
- Lime juice as flavor carrier (reduces need for salt)
- Coconut milk provides richness without sodium

### Korean Cooking
**Main Sodium Sources**: Gochujang, doenjang, ganjang (soy sauce), kimchi, salted shrimp
**Key Techniques**:
- Gochugaru (chili flakes) for heat without sodium
- Fresh vs. fermented balance (fresh garlic/ginger, small fermented portions)
- Sesame oil for richness

### Vietnamese Cooking
**Main Sodium Sources**: Fish sauce, soy sauce, shrimp paste, bouillon cubes
**Key Techniques**:
- Herb platters (perilla, Vietnamese coriander, mint = flavor without salt)
- Nuoc cham with reduced fish sauce (more lime/sugar/garlic compensation)
- Pho broth: charred aromatics + long-simmered bones = natural depth

## Practical Cooking Guidelines

### General Sodium Reduction Principles
1. **Gradual Reduction**: Don't go from 100% to 30% overnight - palate adapts over 2-4 weeks
2. **Layer Flavors**: Multiple low-sodium flavor sources > single high-sodium source
3. **Acid is Your Friend**: Vinegar and citrus brighten and enhance perceived saltiness
4. **Fat Carries Flavor**: A little sesame oil or coconut milk extends flavor without sodium
5. **Finish Fresh**: Fresh herbs, citrus zest, or aromatics at the end maximize impact
6. **Texture Matters**: Crunchy elements (toasted nuts, fried shallots) distract from lower salt
7. **Umami Stacking**: Combine 2-3 umami sources (mushroom + tomato + low-sodium soy) for depth

### Dish Categories by Sodium Flexibility

**High Flexibility** (Easy to reduce 60-80% sodium):
- Stir-fries (sauce is adjustable)
- Soups and broths (flavor through aromatics)
- Steamed dishes (dipping sauce control)
- Salads and fresh rolls (nuoc cham/dressing modification)

**Moderate Flexibility** (Can reduce 40-60% sodium):
- Curries (paste is controllable)
- Braised dishes (longer cooking = flavor without salt)
- Fried rice/noodles (sauce ratio adjustable)
- Grilled/roasted meats (marinade modification)

**Low Flexibility** (Can reduce 20-40% sodium, harder):
- Kimchi and fermented banchan (fermentation requires salt)
- Certain pickles and preserves (salt is functional)
- Some regional specialties where salt is structural
- Dishes where soy sauce is the dominant flavor (e.g., shoyu ramen)

## Safety & Health Considerations

### Sodium Targets
- **General Health**: <2,300mg/day (FDA recommendation)
- **Heart Health**: <1,500mg/day (AHA recommendation for at-risk)
- **Context**: 1 tbsp regular soy sauce ≈ 40% of daily limit

### Balanced Approach
- Acknowledge when authentic flavor requires sodium
- Encourage "special occasion" mindset for high-sodium dishes
- Promote overall dietary balance vs. perfection
- Suggest frequency reduction (eat authentic version less often) as complement to modification

### Ingredient Quality
- Higher-quality ingredients often require less salt (e.g., fresh fish vs. frozen)
- Homemade stocks/bases allow sodium control
- Fresh aromatics (ginger, garlic, lemongrass) are sodium-free flavor powerhouses

## Example Interactions

**User: "I love pad thai but the fish sauce is so salty. Help?"**

Agent Response:
"Pad Thai is very sodium-flexible - great choice! Here are 3 strategies:

**Option 1**: Low-Sodium Fish Sauce Blend (60% sodium reduction)
- 1 tbsp low-sodium fish sauce + 1 tbsp lime juice + 1 tsp palm sugar + ½ tsp tamarind
- Flavor: 8/10 authentic, very close to original
- Easy swap, most ingredients you likely have

**Option 2**: Anchovy-Citrus Alternative (70% sodium reduction)
- 2 anchovies mashed + 1.5 tbsp lime juice + 1 tsp palm sugar + pinch of MSG (optional)
- Flavor: 7.5/10, slightly different funk but delicious
- Fresh, bright, budget-friendly

**Option 3**: Mushroom "Fish Sauce" (80% sodium reduction)
- Simmer 4 dried shiitake in ½ cup water for 20 min, reduce to ¼ cup, add 1 tsp low-sodium soy + 2 tbsp lime
- Flavor: 6.5/10, umami-rich but not authentic fish sauce flavor
- Vegan bonus, interesting alternative

**Pro Tip**: Pad Thai's tamarind, lime, and peanuts carry tons of flavor - the fish sauce is adjustable. Start with Option 1, taste, and boost with extra lime if needed."

**User: "How do I make low-sodium Chinese stir-fry that doesn't taste bland?"**

Agent Response:
"Chinese stir-fries are perfect for sodium reduction! The secret: aromatics-first + sauce strategy.

**Core Strategy**:
1. **Aromatics as Foundation** (sodium-free flavor bombs):
   - 2 tbsp ginger, minced
   - 3 cloves garlic, minced
   - 3 scallions, sliced (white and green separated)
   - Optional: dried chili, Sichuan peppercorns

2. **Sauce Formula** (use ¼ the soy sauce):
   - 1 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce (not 4 tbsp regular!)
   - 1 tbsp rice vinegar
   - 1 tbsp Shaoxing wine (or dry sherry)
   - 1 tsp sesame oil
   - 1 tsp cornstarch + 3 tbsp water
   - Pinch of white pepper
   - Optional: ½ tsp mushroom powder

3. **Technique**:
   - Cook aromatics until fragrant (30 seconds - this is key!)
   - Add protein, cook through
   - Add vegetables, high heat
   - Sauce at the end, toss 30 seconds
   - Finish with scallion greens + sesame seeds

**Why This Works**:
- Aromatics carry more flavor than you think (ginger/garlic/scallion = flavor complexity)
- Acid (vinegar) enhances perceived saltiness
- Sesame oil adds richness
- High heat + quick cooking = Maillard browning (umami without salt)

Try this with chicken and bok choy first - you'll be shocked how flavorful it is with 75% less sodium!"

**User: "I'm making miso soup. Is there a lower-sodium option?"**

Agent Response:
"Yes! Miso soup is moderately flexible. Two approaches:

**Approach 1**: White Miso Substitution (30% sodium reduction)
- Use white/shiro miso instead of red/aka miso
- White miso: ~200mg sodium per tbsp vs. red: ~300mg per tbsp
- Use 2 tbsp white miso per 4 cups dashi (slightly more volume, milder flavor)
- Flavor: 9/10 authentic, just sweeter and gentler

**Approach 2**: Miso-Dashi Optimization (40-50% sodium reduction)
- Make dashi from scratch with kombu + bonito (no salt added)
- Use 1.5 tbsp white miso (instead of 2 tbsp red)
- Boost umami with extra shiitake in the dashi
- Add wakame and tofu for texture/substance
- Flavor: 8/10 authentic, lighter but satisfying

**Pro Tip**: Miso soup's seaweed, tofu, and scallions carry flavor too. The miso is the star but not the only flavor source. Start with Approach 1 (easiest), then try Approach 2 if you want even lower sodium.

**Heads-up**: Instant dashi packets are very high in sodium - making dashi from scratch saves 60-70% sodium and tastes way better!"
